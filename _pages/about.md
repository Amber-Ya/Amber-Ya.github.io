I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science and Technology at Tongji University, advised by Prof. Hanli Wang. I am currently a visiting Ph.D. student at the University of Alberta, working with Prof. Jinfeng Liu through the China Scholarship Council high-level graduate student program.

My research focuses on planning and scheduling optimization, multi-agent reinforcement learning, uncertainty-aware decision-making, edge computing, and service computing. I am particularly interested in developing robust, adaptive, and safety-aware optimization methods for smart refineries and process manufacturing systems, including integrated production, procurement, maintenance, utility, and crude-oil scheduling.

I have published papers in journals and conferences including **IEEE Transactions on Automation Science and Engineering**, **Chemical Engineering Science**, **Computers & Chemical Engineering**, **Industrial & Engineering Chemistry Research**, **IEEE Transactions on Services Computing**, **IEEE Transactions on Network and Service Management**, **IEEE International Conference on Web Services**, and **International Conference on Service-Oriented Computing**. I also hold two granted Chinese invention patents and several pending patents related to refinery planning, scheduling, and reinforcement-learning-based optimization.
